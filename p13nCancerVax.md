# ChatGPT and AlphaFold Help Design Personalized Vaccine for Dog with Cancer
### By TheScientist, Written by Rebecca Roberts, PhD on Mar 18, 2026

https://www.the-scientist.com/chatgpt-and-alphafold-help-design-personalized-vaccine-for-dog-with-cancer-74227
Five Minutes

*When his beloved dog was given months to live, an Australian man harnessed the power of machine learning and mRNA technology to develop a cure.*

An Ausralian man named ***Paul Conyngham***, a data analyst, with Scienist create a personalized mRNA vaccine for is dog ***Rosie*** who have a tennis ball size tumer use **ChatGPT** along with Martin Smith at the **University of News South Wales** and **Pall Thordarson** who is an expert in bio-mimetic chemistry at the UNSW RNA Institute, exame the info Pual made with ChatGPT and Google DeepMind’s AlphaFold to predict the protein structures. to prodict Rosie's cancer cels to creat a personalized vaccien.

Pall Thrdarson explain that ***There was a good level of communication between all of us, Martin, Paul and I, over a couple of months before he'd finished the design, and then as soon as we had the design, we just plugged it into our workflow… We amplified it, and then we made mRNA from that, and we put the mRNA into [lipid nanoparticles] and got it ready for formulation.***

Within two months, Thordarson and his colleagues produced the vaccine while Conyngham worked on the lengthy ethics application to have it administered by Rosie’s veterinarian at the University of Queensland. Conyngham said the change was rapid and remarkable: Within a month, Rosie went from limited mobility to jumping over a fence to chase a rabbit. While not a cure Thordarson said that it *added considerable life and healthspan to Paul's dog Rosie*

Adam Grippin, a physician-scientist at the MD Anderson Cancer Centre and University of Florida, and co-author of the study with Lin commented ***Our technology has the means of making things cheaper right at the manufacturing phase,*** *and that* ***…It's a very exciting time to be in this space, and we're very optimistic about the opportunities to make personalized therapies that are very effective for our patients.***
